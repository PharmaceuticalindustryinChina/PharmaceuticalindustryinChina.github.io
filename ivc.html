The Life of a Drug

The major life stage of a drug includes Research and development, Raw materials manufacturing, and distribution, shown in the graph below. Along the value chain, the key players include pharmaceutical companies, manufacturers, wholesalers, hospitals, pharmacies, and ultimately delivered to end patients.

  1. Research and Development: Initiated with Contract research organizations (CRO), main stages include initial development, preclinical research, and clinical trial
  2. Licensing: Major steps prior to drug licensing include drug registration, quality control, and labeling.
  3. Raw Materials Manufacturing: Drug manufacturing includes both imported and local drugs. For imported drugs, most are in finished dosage forms or bulk packets when shipped, while local manufacturers hired are mainly contract manufacturing organizations.
  4. Distribution: Over the counter (OTC) drugs and prescription drugs are the 2 main categories.

Supply Chain and Major Players

The pharmaceutical industry in China has been one of the fastest growing sectors in the country over the last two decades. Today, the Chinese pharmaceutical market is worth over $110 billion, and ranked as the world’s second largest pharmaceutical market, after the US. This figure is forecasted to grow dramatically to $167 billion by 2020 and further increase its share in the global market. When it comes to pharmaceutical sales, China’s pharmaceutical industry jumped from $21 billion sales in 2008 to over $50 billion in 2012. Several leading foreign pharmaceutical companies are currently dominating the market in China, with surging revenues and a great market over value.

The value chain of China’s pharmaceutical industry includes R&D, Manufacturing, Middlemen distributors, and end distribution.

  • Research and Development is the upstream of the industry supply chain, including pharmaceutical company’s in house R&D team, Contract Research Companies (CRO), and research institutes and universities.
  • Manufacturing industry players include pharmaceutical companies themselves, Contract manufacturing organizations (CMO), and raw material suppliers.
  • Middlemen distributers include wholesalers and E-commerce players, and this part of the industry chain is largely disrupted due to online purchase trends with China’s rapid growth in e-commerce.
  • End consumer distributors include hospitals and pharmacies, providing prescription drugs and OTC drugs to consuemrs.
