Policy & Regulation Support: Drug Administration Law

Impact Across Value Chain

Manufacturing
  • Establishing comprehensive quality control system: vaccine, blood products, narcotic drugs, psychotropic drugs, poisons for medical use are prohibited for outsourced manufacturing unless specified by National Drug Association
  • Parties with Market Authorization (MA) are subject to specifying marketing regulations
Distribution
  • Establishing and reinforcing drug tracking system
  • No online sales for specially administered drugs such as radiative drugs and poisonous drug-chemicals
  • Safety, efficiency and cost-effectiveness as key pillars for the use of drugs for medical institutions
Supervision
  • Parties with MA are subject to post-marketing evaluations on safety, effectiveness and quality controllability of drugs and establishing annual reporting system
  • Parties with conditional MA are subject to post-marketing risk management within pre-specified period

Renewed Incentives

  • Medical value as key orientation
  • Incentivize innovative and child-use drugs
  • Optimize TCM evaluation system
  • Enhance approval process, expert connection
  • Establish check-list system for inventory and shortage management

Stricter Punishment

Distribution Channel

Distribution format

Direct distribution
  • Drugs are directly distributed from pharmaceutical manufacturers to patients
  • Limited to over-the-counter drugs
  • Via pharmaceutical company’s stores
Indirect distribution
  • Drugs go through wholesalers and retailers to reach patients
  • Most common distribution format

Distribution for different drugs

Prescription drugs
  • Based on a physician’s prescription, the drug can be dispensed by a pharmacist to patients
  • Only sales agencies that hold a "drug business license" can deploy and sell to patients
Over-the-counter A drugs
  • Only sales agencies that hold a "drug business license" can deploy and sell to patients
Over-the-counter B drugs
  • Retail pharmacy and approved general stores can deploy and sell to patients

Improvement factors

Development of pharmaceutical companies
  • Choosing a good market selling point is the key to success for pharmaceutical companies. Therefore, they would conduct in-depth researches on the current pharmaceutical distribution channels
  • By establishing an agile and practical distribution channels, the companies will be able to supply and sale drugs to patients on time and fulfill their healthcare needs
Growing pharmaceutical demands
  • Increasing average wages
  • Aging population
  • Enhancing awareness of healthcare
  • Increasing demands of medical quality
  • Improving medical insurance system
Improvement on consumers
  • To resolve issues correlated with retail drug sales, modern technology such as telemarketing, online sales, mail order can build multiple distribution channels
  • By educating the general public to consume drugs more reasonably and promoting related healthcare knowledge, retail pharmacies will be the major distribution channel for generic drugs
Improvement of retail sales
  • Drugs go through wholesalers and retailers to reach patients
  • Standard models for pharmacy chains can establish unified service specifications, unified price, unified management, unified accounting, and unified distribution and purchase
Cancellation of local protection
  • China cancels policies that protects local pharmaceutical markets. This solves the problems of inter-provincial chain operation of pharmaceutical industry and the entry of pharmaceutical companies into rural markets. Also, it creates a more eased environment for the development of pharmaceutical companies
